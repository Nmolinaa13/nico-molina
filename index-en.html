01 — Profile

Lecturer, analyst
and researcher

I am a political scientist specialising in political communication, populist discourse, and democratic erosion in Latin America. My work sits at the intersection of academic rigour and accessible language — I believe political science cannot stay locked inside journals.

I teach at EAFIT, UPB, and UNAL Medellín across electoral systems, political marketing, and empirical methods. I collaborate with the CPOP research group (Political Communication and Public Opinion) at EAFIT and lead projects analysing the Colombian electoral cycle.

In 2026 I begin a PhD in Comparative Politics at the Institute of Social Sciences, Universidade de Lisboa, supervised by Carlos Meléndez and Susana Salgado, focusing on populist discourse and democratic backsliding.

03 — Research

What I write
and why it matters

2026 · Published article
Crowdsourcing platforms for citizenship and democracy in Latin America
Cuadernos.info — No. 64
With Villa Montoya, Restrepo Echavarría & Sepúlveda Cano
Qualitative comparative analysis of 12 crowd-civic systems across 7 Latin American countries (2023–2025). Findings show these platforms operate as flexible participatory infrastructures mediating between citizens, civil society organisations, and public institutions — not as reproductions of normative democratic models.

2026 · Under review
Petro and May Day: Populist discourse 2023–2026
América Latina Hoy
With Freddy Santamaría-Velasco
Quantitative content analysis and LDA topic modelling of four years of presidential speeches delivered on May 1st. Maps the evolution of Petro's populist framing, the construction of "the people," and discursive strategies of institutional confrontation.

2026 · Doctoral proposal
Words that divide, citizenships that resist
ICS-ULisboa · Sup. Carlos Meléndez & Susana Salgado
Doctoral research examining how populist discourse erodes democratic quality in Latin America. Combines computational text analysis, V-Dem data, and comparative case studies in Colombia, Venezuela, and Brazil.

Civic-science tool · Python + Leaflet.js
